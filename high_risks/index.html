Occupational Safety and Health Program


Covered workplaces shall develop and implement a suitable OSH program which shall be posted in prominent places. Covered workplaces shall be guided by a format prescribed by DOLE.

Covered Workplaces

These are workplaces that fall under the jurisdiction of OSH laws (like the Philippines’ RA 11058 and DOLE DO 198-18, or OSHA standards in other countries). Generally, coverage includes:
  • Private companies: All industries regardless of size, unless explicitly exempted.
  • Public sector offices: Government-owned and controlled corporations (GOCCs), sometimes LGUs
  • Contractors/Subcontractors: Both direct employers and project-based operations.
  • High-risk establishments: Construction sites, manufacturing plants, mining, healthcare facilities, and similar industries where hazards are inherent.

Programs

Work Place

The OSH program shall be communicated and be made readily available to all persons in the workplace.

Company

The company shall ensure that the core elements of OSH program are integrated in the company OSH program.

OSH Requirements

The company shall submit a copy of the OSH program to the DOLE Regional, Provincial, Field or Satellite Office having jurisdiction over the workplace.

Program Modification

OSH programs in the pre-approved template may be modified by DOLE as necessary based on existing laws, rules and regulations, and other issuances or upon validation of the program during inspection.

Safety Policies

Written OSH policy statement approved by management.

"Medium risk establishment" refers to a workplace where there is moderate exposure to safety and health hazards and with probability of an accident, injury or illness, if no preventive or control measures are in place.

High Risks Establishments

"High risk establishment" refers to a workplace wherein the presence of hazard or potential hazard within the company may affect the safety and/or health of workers not only within but also persons outside the premises of the workplace (e.g. construction, mining, petrochemical, etc.)

Workplace

It shall be updated periodically whenever the DOLE, other regulatory or government agencies and institutions promulgate new rules, guidelines and other issuances related to workers' safety and health.

Example:

  • Training & Education – Mandatory orientation + regular safety seminars for workers.
  • Health Programs – Pre-employment and periodic medical exams, mental health initiatives, first aid, PPE issuance.
  • Emergency Preparedness – Fire drills, earthquake drills, evacuation plans, first-aid response teams.
  • Accident Reporting & Investigation – Proper documentation, root-cause analysis, and corrective actions.
  • Worker Participation – Safety committees with both employer and worker representatives.
  • Compliance Monitoring – Regular audits, inspections, and submission of reports to DOLE/OSHA.
Company

such as management commitment and employee involvement, workplace risk assessment, hazard prevention and control, safety and health training and education, and OSH program evaluation.

Requirements

A duly signed company commitment to comply OSH requirements together with the company OSH program using the prescribed template shall be considered approved upon submission EXCEPT for Construction Safety and Health Program (CSHP) which shall need approval by DOLE prior to construction.

Program Modification

If there is a modification made by the company, the company shall submit a revised copy of the OSH program to DOLE.


The company shall review and evaluate the OSH program at least once a year or as necessary, to ensure that its objectives are met towards an improved safety and health performance.
